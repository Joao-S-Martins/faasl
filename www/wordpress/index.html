We've built a community around music
and you're invited to join us

Our group was founded in 1980 by amateur musicians who wanted to enrich their community through music. Over 35 years later, our goal remains the same. We remain commited to impacting lives through music education and mentorship. We firmly believe in providing access to musical opportunities and education for people of all walks and stages of life. Music is the thread that intertwines all us all and we want to share it.

Our founders were mostly Portuguese immigrants with varrying musical experience; some with no experience at all. They pooled their knowledge and resources to help eachother and create this band. Today, our all-volunteer organization has become a heterogenous group representing many different social classes and backgrounds. In doing so, we have vastly expanded the pool of experience and resources that we share to better one another.

What a musical community looks like

New musicians are guided every step of the way
by credentialed music teachers

A community has to make a priority of providing for its future generations. We do this with our free beginner and intermediate programs. All lessons are taught in a group setting, with all the same ammenities of our volunteer band. The beginner class introduces new musicians, aged 8+, to their instruments and written music starting each February. The intermediate class is a transitional program whereby students rehearse and perform with our volunteer band, but also have a seperate weekly lesson to focus on excerpts and musical theory.

In our programs, students learn:

  • How to play and maintain their instruments
  • How to read music and keep time
  • Common scales, musical keys, and notations
  • Musical etiquet and how to practice at home

Novice musicians growing
together with experienced musicians

A community must nourish its core. For us, this is our volunteer band. This band is a group of musicians that rehearse weekly and perform on occasions throught the year. It is comprised of musicians young and old, new and experienced, who share a common passion for playing music. The repitiore is updated semi-yearly to push each musician into growing and improving. This challenge encourages all our musicians to help one another, builds commaraderie, and teaches lessons that can't be learned simply by playing in a group of their own peers.

After joining FAASL, I think I've grown as a musician because I have the opportunity to play with more experienced musicians.

Aubury Freed

Public performances
and social events

A community thrives when it shares its talents with others. We work with our contacts in neighbouring communities to maintain a robust performance schedule that takes us around the SF bay and into the central valley every year. These performances give our musicians both a sense of accomplishment and a drive to improve. We host many dinners during the year, organized by the board for the benefit of our music programs. These fundraisers bring classic Portuguese meals to our tables, as well as some American staples. Breaking bread together reaffirms our commitment to our community.
