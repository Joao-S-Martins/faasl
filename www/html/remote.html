We're offering a remote learning opportunity
because COVID-19 can't stop us!

Just because you can't leave your house doesn't mean you can't play music. In fact, the best way to pass this time of seclusion might be to play more music.

Stay home and rekindle your love for playing music.

You know that old instrument tucked away in the back of you closet or gathering dust in the garage? You probably have some great memories of playing it in the past. We want you to make new memories playing it into the future as well.

And We're here to help

We are launching our limited Remote Restart program to help you do just that. We are scheduling one-on-one video sessions right now to help you relearn your instrument. You might only remember how to blow a few notes. You might have forgetten your scales. You might not have any sheet music to play in your house. Maybe you don't even know if your instrument works correctly.

We're here to help you through all of this. During these sessions, we'll field any questions you might have, help you relearn your way around your instrument, provide you with sheet music to practice, and schedule follow-up sessions as needed.

What to expect

To prepare for your session,

  • Print any music we've sent you
  • Find a way to stand the sheet(s) near your laptop or phone
  • Clean your mouthpiece and assemble your instrument
  • Install the video conference software if your device requires it

During your first session, we'll cover

  • Any questions you might have
  • How to get some notes out
  • Review of musical notation, like note lengths and symbols
  • Work through some scales or songs together
  • Discuss practicing alone and schedule a follow-up session

After the session, you should

  • Set your practice schedule as discussed
  • Be bold
  • Make noise
  • Take pride in rediscovering your talent

How to get started

To get started, just send an email to info@faasl.org. Tell us your name, city, the instrument(s) you'd like to play, and a phone number that we can call you at. We'll follow-up with you to arrange a session time. It's just that simple.

There are just a few requirements:

  • Have a wind instrument or drum available to learn (no string instruments or pianos)
  • Have some experience playing music (absolute beginners can join our free lessons when they resume)
  • Reside in one of these San Francisco Bay Area counties: Alameda, Contra Costa, San Mateo, Santa Clara, and San Francisco
Our resources are limited and there are just some cases that we aren't to help at this time. Contact us today

How we can continue to support you
once public gatherings are allowed to resume

At the Portuguese Band of San Leandro, we take a lot of pride in bringing people of all ages and experiences together to play music throughout our communities. Our goal with these Remote Restart sessions is to get more musicians active and performing. As soon as public gatherings are allowed to resume, you'll be invited to sit in at our volunteer band rehearsals or join our always free music lessons.
